Hello judges. Do you know what the most terrifying silent threat is for elderly patients suffering from Sarcopenia? It is not just muscle weakness. It is the vicious cycle known as 'Learned Non-Use'.

When the elderly feel that one arm is weak, they avoid using it and compensate with their dominant arm. Over time, the brain literally 'forgets' how to control the weak arm, leading to loss of balance and critical fall risks. 

The question is, how do we know if a patient reaching for a cup is truly using their dominant arm, or simply compensating because the other arm suffers from Learned Non-Use? This is the exact challenge we are solving today.

Welcome to KineTwin, a Kinematic Digital Twin system for detecting Learned Non-Use. Our innovation integrates AI Computer Vision with Interactive 3D WebGL. Using the 'AI Vibe Coding' approach, we developed this system alongside an AI Agent to transform ordinary webcam pixels into precise Digital Biomarkers, completely eliminating the need for expensive sensors.

On the left is a video of a patient performing a target-reaching task. On the right, our highlight: The Reference Hologram. The system renders a real-time, ergonomically perfect 3D skeleton. We can orbit and inspect the movement trajectory in full 360 degrees.

Our AI extracts 21 joint landmarks from the patient's video and compares them against this Hologram to analyze three crucial metrics: First, Speed, to measure hesitation. Second, Accuracy, to measure the straightness of the reach. And third, Quality of Movement, to detect tremors or smoothness. If deviations or compensatory movements are found, the system immediately flags the arm for Learned Non-Use risk.

In the future, KineTwin will be a highly accessible Web Application. Patients simply open their webcam at home and perform the tasks. The analyzed data is instantly sent to physical therapists.
